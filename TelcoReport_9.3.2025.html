Introduction

Customer churn is when a customer stops using a companys service, a major challenge in the telecom industry. Retaining customers is critical because acquiring a new customer often costs significantly more than keeping an existing one. High churn rates can directly impact revenue, market share, and long-term profitability, making churn analysis an essential part of business strategy.

This project focuses on analyzing a telecom customer dataset to better understand the factors that influence churn. The dataset contains information about customer demographics, services subscribed to, contract types, payment methods, and whether the customer ultimately churned. Data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and visualization, uncovers meaningful patterns and trends related to customer behavior.

The key objectives of this analysis are to:

  • Identify major drivers of churn in the telecom dataset.
  • Explore the relationships between services, contracts, payment methods, and churn.
  • Provide actionable insights that could inform retention strategies.

The report is organized into the following sections:

  1. Methodology Outlines the data cleaning and analytical approach.
  2. Analysis Presents the findings from exploratory data analysis.
  3. Insights Summarizes the key takeaways from the analysis.
  4. Recommendations Suggests potential business strategies to reduce churn.
  5. Conclusions Provides a final summary of findings and their implications.

Methodology

To ensure the dataset was reliable and ready for analysis, the project followed a structured methodology consisting of two main stages: data cleaning and exploratory data analysis.

1. Data Cleaning

Data cleaning was performed to prepare the dataset for accurate analysis. The key steps included:

  • Handling missing values: Identified and addressed null or inconsistent entries.
  • Data type adjustments: Converted columns (e.g., categorical and numerical features) into appropriate formats for analysis.
  • Feature engineering: Created new variables where relevant (e.g., total number of services, AutoPay indicator) to capture additional customer behavior insights.
  • Standardization of categorical values: Ensured consistent labeling across service and contract features.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA was conducted to uncover initial patterns, distributions, and relationships within the data. This included:

  • Descriptive statistics: Summarized numerical and categorical features to understand customer demographics and service usage.
  • Churn distribution: Examined the overall churn rate and compared it across different customer groups.
  • Service usage patterns: Analyzed how the number and type of subscribed services correlated with churn.
  • Contract and payment analysis: Investigated churn rates by contract type, payment method, and AutoPay status.
  • Visualizations: Employed bar charts and churn rate comparisons to highlight significant trends.

This systematic approach ensured that the dataset was clean, reliable, and ready for extracting meaningful insights into customer churn behavior.

    "DeviceProtection":  {0:"No", 1:"Yes", 2:"No internet service"},
    "TechSupport":       {0:"No", 1:"Yes", 2:"No internet service"},
    "StreamingTV":       {0:"No", 1:"Yes", 2:"No internet service"},
    "StreamingMovies":   {0:"No", 1:"Yes", 2:"No internet service"},
}

# 3) Build a tidy table: Service | Level | Customers | ChurnRate%

rows = []
for col in service_all:
    s = data[col]
    if col in maps:
        s = s.map(maps[col]) # convert 0/1/2 -> strings
    s = s.astype("string").fillna("Unknown") # ensure strings everywhere

    tmp = (
        data.assign(_level=s)
          .groupby("_level", dropna=False)["Churn"]
          .agg(ChurnRate=lambda x: x.mean()*100, Customers="count")
          .reset_index()
          .rename(columns={"_level":"Level"})
    )
    tmp.insert(0, "Service", col)
    rows.append(tmp)

churn_by_service_long = pd.concat(rows, ignore_index=True)

Insights

The analysis identified four primary drivers of churn in the telecom dataset:

  1. Demographics
    Senior citizens without dependents or partners have the highest churn rate, approaching 50%. This highlights that customer demographics play a significant role in retention, particularly for single senior households.

  2. Contract Type
    Customers on month-to-month contracts experience a churn rate of 43%. Among the targeted demographic group, 454 out of 561 used month-to-month contracts, underscoring the lack of long-term commitment as a critical factor in customer attrition.

  3. Value-Added Services (Online Security and Tech Support)
    Two optional services emerged as strong churn differentiators:

    • Customers without Online Security churn at a rate about 27% higher than those with it.
    • Customers without Tech Support churn about 26% more than those with it.
    • Only 105 out of 561 retain online security.
    • Only 98 out of 561 retain tech support.

    This shows that service engagement strongly influences retention among this targeted group.

  4. Payment Method and AutoPay
    Customers not enrolled in AutoPay churn at more than double the rate (35% vs. 16%). Payment method also matters: customers paying by electronic check have the highest churn rate at 45% and the highest adoption rate; None of these customers are enrolled in AutoPay. Billing convenience and method are, therefore, major churn drivers.

Recommendations

Based on the identified churn drivers, the following strategies are recommended:

  1. Targeted Support for At-Risk Demographics

    • Develop specialized retention programs for senior customers without dependents or partners, such as personalized customer service, simplified plan options, and loyalty rewards.
    • Provide tailored communication and outreach that builds trust and emphasizes reliability, which may resonate more strongly with this group.

  2. Promote Long-Term Contracts

    • Incentivize customers to switch from month-to-month to one- or two-year contracts through discounts, bundled offers, or loyalty benefits.
    • Highlight the value of stability and potential cost savings in marketing campaigns to encourage longer commitments.

  3. Increase Adoption of Online Security and Tech Support

    • Bundle Online Security and Tech Support with core services at a reduced cost to make them more attractive.
    • Educate customers on the benefits of these services, emphasizing protection, reliability, and peace of mind.
    • Target the specified group of customers specifically, since their adoption of these services is currently lowest.

  4. Encourage AutoPay Enrollment and Improve Payment Options

    • Offer incentives such as discounts, reward points, or exclusive perks for customers who enroll in AutoPay.
    • Improve awareness of AutoPay benefits through customer communication.
    • Reduce reliance on electronic checks by encouraging digital payment methods (credit/debit cards, bank transfers) that integrate seamlessly with AutoPay.

Implementing these strategies can directly address the key drivers of churn. By focusing on customer demographics, contract structures, service adoption, and billing methods, the company can strengthen customer loyalty and significantly reduce churn.

Conclusion

This project analyzed telecom customer data to identify the major drivers of churn and provide actionable strategies for retention. The analysis revealed four primary churn factors: customer demographics, contract type, adoption of value-added services, and billing/payment methods.

Key findings show that senior citizens without dependents or partners are at the highest risk of churn, especially when combined with month-to-month contracts and low adoption of support services. Additionally, reliance on electronic checks and lack of AutoPay enrollment strongly correlate with elevated churn rates.

These insights suggest that churn is not driven by a single factor but by an interplay of customer demographics, service engagement, and billing practices. Addressing these areas through targeted retention strategiesm such as promoting long-term contracts, incentivizing AutoPay, and increasing adoption of security and support services can significantly reduce churn.

Ultimately, by focusing on the needs of vulnerable customer segments and removing friction in service and payment experiences, the company can strengthen customer loyalty, improve profitability, and build a more resilient customer base.
